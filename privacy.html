1 Overview

SaveUp does not collect, transmit, or store any of your personal or financial data on external servers. Everything lives on your device.

SaveUp is an offline-first personal finance app for Android and Windows. All budgets, transactions, savings goals, and user profiles are stored locally in a SQLite database on your own device. We have no servers, no accounts, and no cloud infrastructure.

This Privacy Policy explains what limited data is accessed by the app and why, and how third-party libraries used in the app may behave.

2 What data we collect

We do not collect any data. All information you enter into SaveUp — transactions, budgets, account balances, goals, group names, and user names — is stored exclusively in a local SQLite database on your device.

  • No account registration required
  • No email address, phone number, or identity information collected
  • No analytics or crash reporting sent to our servers
  • No advertising identifiers used
  • No data sold or shared with third parties by us

Profile photos you choose to add are stored only in the app's private storage directory on your device and are never uploaded anywhere.

3 App permissions

SaveUp requests the following permissions on Android:

📷
CAMERA
Used only to scan QR codes when joining a group. The camera is never accessed in the background and no images are saved or transmitted.
🖼️
READ_MEDIA_IMAGES / READ_EXTERNAL_STORAGE
Used only when you choose to set a profile photo from your gallery. The selected image is copied to the app's private storage and the original is not modified.
🔔
POST_NOTIFICATIONS
Used to send local notifications when recurring transactions are generated or when a budget limit is reached. No notifications are sent from external servers.
📶
ACCESS_NETWORK_STATE / CHANGE_NETWORK_STATE
Used only to detect whether you are connected to a Wi-Fi network, which is required for the local Wi-Fi Sync feature. No internet connections are made by SaveUp itself.
⏰
RECEIVE_BOOT_COMPLETED / WAKE_LOCK / SCHEDULE_EXACT_ALARM
Used by WorkManager to run background tasks that generate recurring transactions and check budget progress. These tasks run entirely on-device.

4 Wi-Fi Sync

SaveUp includes an optional Wi-Fi Sync feature that lets two or more devices on the same local network exchange financial data directly — without any internet connection or intermediary server.

  • Sync only happens when you explicitly initiate it from the Sync screen
  • Data travels only between devices on your local Wi-Fi network
  • No data is sent to the internet or to our servers at any point
  • mDNS (Bonjour/Zeroconf) is used for local device discovery

Wi-Fi Sync is peer-to-peer and local only. Your financial data never leaves your home or office network.

5 Third-party SDKs

SaveUp uses a small number of open-source libraries. Most have no data collection. One exception is noted below:

Google ML Kit — Barcode Scanning

Used for QR code scanning (joining a group). ML Kit may send anonymous usage statistics to Google via Firebase Transport. This telemetry is controlled by Google, not by SaveUp. You can review Google's privacy practices at policies.google.com/privacy.

Flutter framework (Google)

The app is built with Flutter. The framework itself does not collect data. See flutter.dev/privacy.

All other libraries (sqflite, provider, fl_chart, workmanager, etc.) are local utilities with no network activity or data collection.

6 Children's privacy

SaveUp does not knowingly collect any personal information from children under the age of 13. The app does not require account creation and collects no personal data from any user, regardless of age.
